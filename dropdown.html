Dropdown character selector

After much anticipation, Duolingo recently released their Japanese beta course to desktop users following a redesign of their website to be more consistent with their mobile app.

This was met with mixed reviews since the redesign bought in the somewhat unpopular tile selection exercises where the user is asked to translate a phrase and are given a limited set of words to choose from in order to do so.

I don't particularly mind it but I understand why it's unpopular (offers the user less motivation/opportunity to recall) and also why the people behind Duolingo may have gone down this route (better parity with their mobile app means, in future, courses can be made for one and work for both. Also, I suspect they may anticipate that some people could be put off/otherwise hindered by the 'entry requirement' of having to download the target language keyboard)

Here I have a simple demo of a possible solution to the latter issue [Github link].

How to use this thing

Type the pronunciation of a Japanese character (for example, 'tsu') into the input field and can select from a list of matching Japanese characters (for 'tsu' the options would be ツ, ッ, つ, っ) by using the up/down arrow keys, pressing enter once you find what you're looking for.
To access つ one may type '/tsu' or just 'ts' followed by two presses of the down arrow key or even 't' followed by six presses of the down arrow key and then press enter.

With the exception of ッ, っ, and ー (which, here, are accessed with 'tsu','tsu' and '-' respectively) all the pronunciation pairs can be found on my character tooltips page.
